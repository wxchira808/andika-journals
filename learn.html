A letter from the founder

Why I Created The Wise Man Journal

A journal created from prayer, compassion, and the belief that strength and honesty have always belonged together.

When God first placed this journal on my heart, I honestly couldn’t believe it. Out of all the things I thought I would do, creating a journal for men was not one of them. Yet the idea stayed with me. I prayed about it, cried over it, and eventually came to God with a simple prayer: “Lord, I step aside. Let this be Yours. Speak to the men who need these words.” Because this was never about me having all the answers. It was about creating something that would help men feel seen, understood, and reminded that they do not have to carry life alone.

Over the years, I have watched the men around me carry burdens that often go unnoticed. I’ve seen fathers quietly worrying about finances while trying to provide for their families. I’ve seen husbands carrying responsibilities they struggle to explain. I’ve seen young men questioning their purpose and wondering if they are falling behind. I’ve seen men battle loneliness, disappointment, grief, anger, temptation, and fears they rarely speak about. I’ve seen men who felt they had to stay strong for everyone around them, even when they themselves were exhausted. And perhaps what has touched my heart most is how rarely anyone stops and asks them how they are really doing. Not what they do. Not what they earn. But how they are beneath the responsibilities and expectations they carry every day.

I believe men have hearts. I believe they feel deeply, love deeply, and carry far more than most people will ever know. And I believe men need space to think, to reflect, to pray, and to ask the questions they may have been carrying for years. They need room to talk about purpose, relationships, faith, fears, disappointments, and the kind of men they hope to become. Because strength and honesty have always belonged together.

That is why I created The Wise Man Journal. Not to fix men, because men are not projects. And not to add more pressure to lives that are already heavy. I created it to be a companion. A quiet place to sit with yourself and with God. A place to grow in wisdom, strengthen your faith, and become more intentional in the way you live. Through these pages, my hope is that men will understand themselves more deeply, find peace in the middle of life’s noise, and discover that growth is not about perfection. It is about becoming more honest, more grounded, and more aware of the man God is shaping them to be.

More than anything, my prayer is that every man who opens this journal will know that he is not alone. I pray he knows that making mistakes does not disqualify him, that needing rest does not make him weak, and that God sees him even in the battles nobody else knows about. And if these pages help a man draw closer to God, understand himself more deeply, and become the man God created him to be, then every prayer, every tear, and every moment spent creating this journal will have been worth it.
